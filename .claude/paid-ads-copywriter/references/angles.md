# Angle Library for Paid Ads

An "angle" is the strategic lens through which you present the product. Same product, different
angle = completely different ad. Test angles before testing copy variations within an angle.

---

## Core Angle Categories

### 1. Problem / Pain
Lead with the frustration. Make the reader feel seen before introducing the solution.

**Best for:** Cold traffic, high-awareness problem categories, commoditized markets
**Hook patterns:**
- "Tired of [symptom]?"
- "If [painful situation], you're not alone."
- "Most [audience] deal with [problem] for years before finding this."
- "The reason [common solution] isn't working for you."

**Watch out for:** Over-agitating without relief. Pain without hope = scroll past.

---

### 2. Outcome / Dream State
Lead with the desirable result. Skip the problem entirely. Paint the life after.

**Best for:** Aspiration-heavy categories (beauty, fitness, lifestyle), warm audiences
**Hook patterns:**
- "Imagine [specific outcome] in [timeframe]."
- "What would you do with [benefit]?"
- "[Number] people are already [achieving outcome]."
- "This is what [result] actually looks like."

**Watch out for:** Vague outcomes ("feel amazing"). Specificity is everything here.

---

### 3. Social Proof / Credibility
Lead with numbers, testimonials, endorsements, or awards. Let others sell for you.

**Best for:** Mid-funnel, skeptical audiences, saturated markets, new brand building trust
**Hook patterns:**
- "[X,000] customers can't be wrong."
- "Rated [X] stars by [X] verified buyers."
- "The [product] that [media outlet] called '[quote].'"
- "[Influencer/expert] said this changed everything for them."

**Watch out for:** Vague social proof ("thousands love it"). Specific numbers outperform.

---

### 4. Curiosity / Pattern Interrupt
Say something unexpected. Break the visual or logical pattern of the feed.

**Best for:** Cold audiences, creative fatigue situations, commodity products needing
differentiation
**Hook patterns:**
- "Nobody tells you [counterintuitive truth]."
- "The [thing everyone does] is making your [problem] worse."
- "We did [unexpected thing] so you don't have to."
- "Stop [common advice]. Do this instead."

**Watch out for:** Clickbait that the product can't back up. The reveal must deliver.

---

### 5. Objection Flip
Lead with the #1 reason people don't buy — then dismantle it.

**Best for:** High-consideration purchases, skeptical audiences, retargeting
**Hook patterns:**
- "Think [product category] doesn't work? Read this."
- "Yes, we're more expensive. Here's why that saves you money."
- "No time? This takes 30 seconds a day."
- "If you've tried [competitor/approach] and failed, here's why."

**Watch out for:** Raising objections you can't credibly answer. Only flip what you can win.

---

### 6. Mechanism / Unique Mechanism
Lead with HOW it works — not WHAT it does. The mechanism is the differentiator.

**Best for:** Science-backed products, sophisticated audiences, categories with many
competitors making similar claims
**Hook patterns:**
- "The reason [product type] usually fails? [Problem with category]. We fixed that."
- "Most [products] do X. Ours does Y instead — here's why it matters."
- "[Ingredient/process] is why [X] customers switched from [competitor category]."

**Watch out for:** Getting too technical. Keep the mechanism simple enough to visualize.

---

### 7. Identity / Tribe
Lead with who the customer is (or wants to be). The product is an expression of identity.

**Best for:** Lifestyle brands, community-oriented products, premium positioning
**Hook patterns:**
- "For people who [value or belief statement]."
- "This is for [identity group] who refuse to [compromise]."
- "If you're the kind of person who [behavior], you'll get it."

**Watch out for:** Exclusion that alienates more than it attracts on cold traffic.

---

### 8. Urgency / Scarcity
Create a real reason to act now. Time, quantity, or price-limited.

**Best for:** Retargeting, promotional campaigns, launch periods
**Hook patterns:**
- "Last chance: [offer] ends [date]."
- "Only [X] left at this price."
- "We do this once a year. It's happening now."

**Watch out for:** Fake urgency. If it's not real, sophisticated buyers ignore it and
lose trust. Only use when the urgency is genuine.

---

### 9. Story / Founder / Origin
Lead with narrative. Why does this product exist? Who made it and why?

**Best for:** Brand building, authenticity plays, founder-led brands, DTC
**Hook patterns:**
- "I built this because [personal story]."
- "After [years/struggle], I finally figured out [insight]."
- "The [profession/expert] who couldn't find a product that worked — so they built one."

**Watch out for:** Stories that go too long before the payoff. Hook first, story second.

---

## Angle Selection Matrix

| Audience Temperature | Recommended Primary Angle | Secondary to Test |
|---|---|---|
| Cold (no awareness) | Problem/Pain or Curiosity | Outcome, Mechanism |
| Warm (engaged, no purchase) | Social Proof or Objection Flip | Story, Outcome |
| Hot (retargeting cart/LP visitors) | Urgency or Objection Flip | Social Proof |
| Existing customers (upsell) | Identity or Outcome | Story |

---

## Angle Testing Protocol

1. **Pick 3–5 angles** from different categories (not variations of the same theme)
2. **Test one variable at a time** — same angle, different hook = hook test; different angle = angle test
3. **Run for 3–5 days minimum** before drawing conclusions (longer for low-volume campaigns)
4. **Kill losers fast, scale winners** — allocate budget to the angle that wins on CPM-adjusted
   metrics (CPC, CTR, cost per purchase)
5. **Don't kill a losing angle** — revisit with a different hook. The angle may be right, the
   execution wrong.
